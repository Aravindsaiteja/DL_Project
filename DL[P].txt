Explainability Analysis of Neural Networks Using Feature Attribution Methods


Abstract
Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) have demonstrated outstanding performance across a wide range of applications; however, their decision-making processes remain largely opaque. This lack of interpretability limits their adoption in sensitive and high-stakes domains such as healthcare, finance, and autonomous systems. This project focuses on analyzing the explainability of neural networks using feature attribution methods to uncover how individual input features contribute to model predictions.
The proposed approach evaluates multiple attribution techniques including saliency-based, gradient-based, and model-agnostic methods to interpret trained neural network models. Experiments will be conducted on standard benchmark datasets to compare explanation quality, stability, and faithfulness. The results will be analyzed through visualizations and quantitative metrics to highlight the strengths and limitations of each method. This study aims to enhance trust, transparency, and accountability in deep learning models by providing systematic insights into their internal decision mechanisms.
